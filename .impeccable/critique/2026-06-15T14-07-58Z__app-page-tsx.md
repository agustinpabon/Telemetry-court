#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** This does not look like a throwaway AI dashboard, but it does still carry a recognizable 2026 "AI cyber analytics" scent: dark cosmic panel, cyan/purple glow, starfield, orbital rings, tiny technical legend, and floating telemetry tooltip. The strongest premium product moment is the right-side case dossier; the weakest moment is the atlas viewport trying to be both data instrument and cinematic space scene.

The largest slop tell is not one component; it is accumulation. The UI says "calm scientific evidence arena," but the viewport says "galactic threat map." The result is impressive at first glance and less trustworthy after ten seconds, because evidence hierarchy is less legible than atmosphere.

#### Overall Impression

The Evidence Galaxy Atlas has a real product idea and a memorable first impression. It is already more distinctive than a generic dashboard grid. The single biggest opportunity is to make it feel less like a beautiful sci-fi map and more like a premium cyber-analytics instrument where evidence state, selected case context, and next action are unmistakable.

#### What's Working

- The right selected-region dossier is the strongest product object. It has a clear case title, claim under test, rationale, signals, and one primary action.
- The synthetic evidence model is visible in the UI. Session count, support, uncertainty, status, and candidate regions are not decorative labels; they map to real fixture fields.
- The structured-choice posture is intact. The first screen leads toward opening a case file rather than typing into a chatbot.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] Evidence hierarchy is split between three competing selected-case surfaces**

**Why it matters:** The selected case appears as a node chip, a floating "Selected evidence" tooltip inside the map, and the large right dossier. The right dossier should be the source of truth, but the floating summary visually sits in the middle of the atlas and competes with node selection. Reviewers have to decide where to look before they can decide what the evidence says.

**Fix:** Remove the floating summary or make it a tiny anchored lens that only appears on hover/focus. Keep persistent selected-case details in the right dossier. Collapse the map key into a quieter legend strip or an explicit "Map encodings" disclosure outside the data field.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout components/arena/EvidenceGalaxyAtlas.tsx`

**[P1] The atlas drifts toward generic AI cyber-map aesthetics**

**Why it matters:** The product explicitly rejects cyberpunk/SOC-wall styling, but the current viewport uses a dark cosmic field, star drift, orbital rings, cyan/purple glow, and tiny instrumentation. It is polished, but it risks reading as a familiar AI-generated cybersecurity visual rather than a high-trust evidence product.

**Fix:** Keep the spatial metaphor but translate it toward a scientific evidence plate: fewer starfield layers, fewer glow colors, stronger neutral material, clearer region boundaries, and semantic encodings that look measured rather than cinematic. The map can stay dark, but it needs restraint and evidence-first contrast.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable quieter components/arena/EvidenceGalaxyAtlas.tsx`

**[P1] Selection and interaction states are under-explained**

**Why it matters:** Nodes are clickable and have ARIA labels, but visually the difference between hover, preview, selected, linked, and distant is subtle because every state uses some mix of glow, opacity, orbit, and labels. The explicit `.cluster-node-selected-label` is hidden in CSS, so a selected node does not get a simple persistent textual marker.

**Fix:** Give selected state one stable, obvious treatment: persistent selected label, stronger outline, selected path focus, and a matching dossier header state. Make preview temporary and lighter. Keep keyboard arrow navigation, but add a small visible hint or make the stage/atlas focus state more legible.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable polish components/arena/ClusterNode.tsx`

**[P2] Micro-typography is too small in the densest areas**

**Why it matters:** The map key uses 8.5-10px labels, the floating summary uses 8-11px text, and dark translucent surfaces lower perceived contrast. This makes the most explanatory text the hardest to read. It also feels more like a dashboard HUD than a premium product UI.

**Fix:** Raise legend and summary text to a readable 11-12px minimum, reduce the number of legend rows visible at once, and move explanatory copy out of the map when it is not directly needed. Keep body copy in the right dossier as the readable source.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable typeset components/arena/EvidenceGalaxyAtlas.tsx`

**[P2] The CSS override stack is now influencing design quality**

**Why it matters:** The atlas has multiple historical visual layers in `app/globals.css`: light atlas rules, dark atlas rules, observation-window refinements, and final overrides. That makes future visual choices fragile and increases the chance of inconsistent mode behavior across breakpoints.

**Fix:** Consolidate the active atlas CSS into one canonical block after design direction is chosen. Keep the active component vocabulary, delete superseded rules, and encode the light shell/dark atlas relationship as an intentional mode rather than cascade archaeology.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable extract app/globals.css`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User):** The core path is clickable, but expert acceleration is weak. Arrow navigation exists on the atlas container, yet the UI does not advertise it. The stage rail is visually reduced to dots in explore mode, so power users lose fast orientation. The selected case can be changed, but batch comparison or quick scan across cases is not surfaced.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User):** The atlas buttons are real buttons with ARIA labels, which is good. The risk is visual-state dependence: status tint, glow, opacity, and ring behavior carry meaning that the visible text does not always reinforce. The smallest legend and summary text are likely uncomfortable at zoom or low vision, and the selected label exists in markup but is hidden visually.

**Jordan (First-Timer):** The first screen asks Jordan to understand "Telemetry Galaxy," "Semantic evidence atlas," "Review JSON," "semantic adjacency," and "ring instability" before the first case action. The right dossier eventually clarifies the task, but the top-right `Review JSON` action may look like the primary thing to do even before any review exists.

#### Minor Observations

- The top-level H1 is the tagline, while the visible page title repeats "Semantic evidence atlas"; the brand/product identity could be stronger.
- `Review JSON` is available immediately, but it is a late-stage artifact. It should probably be quieter until meaningful review state exists.
- The selected-region accent stripe is a banned side-accent pattern in the Impeccable rules. Replace it with a full border tint, small status glyph, or header treatment.
- The right dossier's metric row wraps into stacked words at this width, which hurts the premium feel despite the data being useful.
- The map key is helpful, but six encodings plus five status chips is too much persistent explanatory UI inside the map.

#### Questions to Consider

- What if the map became a quieter evidence plate and the right dossier became the unmistakable command surface?
- Does the first screen need `Review JSON`, or should the late-stage export stay behind the case workflow until a verdict exists?
- Which three encodings must be legible without explanation: support, uncertainty, status, or semantic adjacency?
- What would make this feel like a premium cyber-analytics product without using the expected cyber palette?
