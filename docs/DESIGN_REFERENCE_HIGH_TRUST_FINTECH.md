# Design Reference: High-Trust Fintech

## Purpose

This document distills useful high-trust fintech design principles for Telemetry Court. It is not an instruction to clone Wealthsimple.

The source research is useful because it describes a calm, premium, trust-oriented product grammar: visual restraint, low noise, generous spacing, clear hierarchy, direct copy, and careful legal boundaries. Telemetry Court should translate those principles into an evidence-first visual language for reviewing AI-generated telemetry interpretations.

## Useful Observations

- Visual restraint keeps attention on the task.
- Trust-signalling clarity matters more than decorative polish.
- Editorial warmth can make serious products feel approachable without becoming casual.
- Large negative space makes complex information easier to inspect.
- Soft pastel or warm neutral fields can create calm separation.
- Utilitarian product cards work well for task-focused review surfaces.
- Near-white surfaces and muted greys reduce visual fatigue.
- Rounded containers can feel premium when paired with strong hierarchy.
- Thin monochrome icon style keeps controls quiet.
- Clear data hierarchy matters more than data density.
- Sparse interaction paths help users complete sensitive tasks with confidence.

## What Telemetry Court Should Borrow

Telemetry Court should borrow restraint, spacing, warm neutral surfaces, direct plainspoken copy, low-noise product UI, clear task flows, rounded cards, strong contrast, and semantic status accents.

These are structural design principles, not brand assets.

## What Telemetry Court Must Not Borrow

Do not borrow Wealthsimple's logo, wordmark, slogans, screenshots, exact layouts, exact product flows, illustrations, icons, 3D render style, brand names, or copy.

Do not make Telemetry Court look like it belongs to Wealthsimple or any other existing brand. Do not reproduce protected brand expression.

## Translation to Telemetry Court

High-trust fintech concepts map to Telemetry Court concepts like this:

- Portfolio/account overview -> case overview.
- Account card -> cluster card.
- Transaction/activity row -> evidence item row.
- Performance indicator -> support score.
- Risk disclosure -> interpretation risk.
- Transfer/deposit flow clarity -> claim review flow clarity.
- Profile/trust settings -> audit trail / analyst verdict.

## Design Implications

Evidence is the asset being inspected. Claims are the positions under review. Scores are not decorative metrics; they are judgments that need rationale. The UI should make it easy to challenge the AI label.

This means visual hierarchy should always privilege case context, AI-generated label or explanation, extracted claims, linked evidence, support score, analyst verdict, and missing evidence. Styling that does not improve this inspection flow should be avoided.

## Legal-Safe Boundary

This document is for design principles only. Do not reproduce protected brand expression.

Use Wealthsimple-style research only as structural design inspiration for calm spacing, restrained color, clear hierarchy, direct copy, and trust-signalling clarity. Telemetry Court must retain its own identity, vocabulary, evidence model, interaction patterns, visual details, and product purpose.
